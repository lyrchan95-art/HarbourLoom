About Dhaka

Dhaka, the capital of Bangladesh, is a vibrant megacity known for its rich history, bustling streets, and warm-hearted people. Founded in the 17th century, it has grown into one of the world's most densely populated cities, serving as the political, economic, and cultural hub of the country.

Located on the banks of the Buriganga River, Dhaka is famous for its Mughal and British colonial architecture, vibrant rickshaw art, and as a center for the world's textile industry.

With a population exceeding 21 million in the metropolitan area, Dhaka is a city of contrasts where ancient traditions coexist with modern development, creating a unique urban tapestry that captivates visitors from around the world.

Dhaka Highlights

Lalbagh Fort

An incomplete 17th-century Mughal fort complex that stands as an emblem of the city's historical significance.

Ahsan Manzil

The former official residential palace of the Nawab of Dhaka, known as the Pink Palace for its distinctive color.

Sadarghat River Port

The bustling river port on the Buriganga River, offering a glimpse into Dhaka's vibrant riverine culture.

Culture & Heritage

Rickshaw Art

Dhaka is famous for its elaborately decorated cycle rickshaws, which serve as moving galleries of folk art. These vibrant paintings depict everything from Bollywood stars to rural landscapes, making each rickshaw a unique piece of mobile art.

Culinary Delights

Dhaka's food scene reflects its diverse cultural influences. From street food like fuchka and chotpoti to traditional Bengali feasts with ilish (hilsa fish) and biryani, the city offers an unforgettable culinary journey for food enthusiasts.

Textile Heritage

As the heart of Bangladesh's garment industry, Dhaka plays a crucial role in global fashion. The city is also known for its traditional muslin fabric, jamdani weaving, and nakshi kantha embroidery, preserving ancient textile traditions.

Plan Your Visit

Dhaka welcomes visitors with its unique blend of history, culture, and urban energy. The best time to visit is between October and March when the weather is cooler and more pleasant for exploration.

Must-see attractions include the historic Old Dhaka, the National Parliament Building, and the bustling markets of New Market and Bashundhara City.
